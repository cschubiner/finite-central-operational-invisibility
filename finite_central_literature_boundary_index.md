# Literature Boundary Index: Finite Central Operational Invisibility

Status: non-frozen reviewer-facing literature map. This file does not change
the frozen theorem, manuscript, Lean core, archives, hashes, manifest, or
external-review request.

## Purpose

Give an outside referee a compact map of the exact prior-art boundaries that
can kill, demote, or preserve the finite-central operational invisibility
claim. This is not a priority claim. It records where the package should be
attacked first.

## Current Claim To Test

The package claims a joint finite-central descent criterion:

- represented, retracted, restricted, generated-algebra, or tested operational
  data determine full-algebra targets exactly when the corresponding finite
  central separator, tensor-kernel separator, sector-character datum,
  quotient/exclusion rule, or entropy/area calibration is supplied;
- without that matching datum, and when the comparison class contains the
  displayed same-data finite-central pair, no reconstruction rule from the
  supplied operational data determines the target.

If an existing theorem already states this exact joint criterion, the novelty
claim should be demoted before the theorem surface is changed.

## Most Dangerous Demotions

1. **Known central-support theorem plus trivial finite linear algebra.** A
   reviewer may say the joint criterion is useful exposition but not a new
   theorem. If so, demote novelty and cite the exact standard sources.
2. **Split/tensor row too broad.** A reviewer may show that the stated
   generated-algebra assumptions already include a spatial tensor-product exit.
   If so, narrow or repair that row.
3. **Entropy coefficient needs an analytic area-limit hypothesis.** If the
   entropy-density row needs a stronger trace/area comparison premise, add it
   only after recording the written response.
4. **Lean abstraction mismatch.** If the finite kernel is too weak or too broad
   relative to the manuscript's standard-language theorem, patch the boundary
   prose or theorem statement narrowly.

## What Would Preserve The Claim

The package survives as a narrow referee-grade contribution only if outside
review confirms all three points:

- no standard theorem already states the same joint finite-central operational
  descent criterion;
- no hypothesis already present in the manuscript rules out the finite central
  same-data pairs;
- the Lean finite kernel faithfully captures the same-data/no-reconstruction
  core after the cited analytic/operator-algebra hypotheses reduce the problem
  to finite central fibers.

Even then, the correct claim remains negative and conditional. It is not a
black-hole entropy derivation, not a binary-rank theorem, and not a new
reconstruction theorem for any of the cited frameworks.
