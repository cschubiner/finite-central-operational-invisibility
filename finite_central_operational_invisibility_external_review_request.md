# External Review Request: Finite Central Operational Invisibility

## Request

Please review the short note
`docs/finite_central_operational_invisibility_short_note.pdf` as a cold
mathematical-physics manuscript.  The intended standards are modest but strict:
the main claim should be either a new finite-central converse theorem, a known
result in disguise, or a false statement with a clear counterexample.

## Main Claim To Attack

The note claims a joint finite-central descent criterion.  For represented,
retracted, restricted, generated-algebra, or tested operational data, the
following full-algebra targets are reconstructible exactly when the matching
separating or calibrating datum is supplied:

- full central support;
- atoms and weights of a specified finite center;
- finite central cardinality, including the two-atom predicate;
- factor-valuedness of a retained local algebra;
- unquotiented spatial tensor-product coordinates;
- finite central channel dynamics;
- central-character labels in supplied sector data;
- absolute finite-center entropy terms and the horizon entropy-density
  coefficient.

If the matching datum is absent and the comparison class contains the displayed
finite-central same-data pair, the note claims that no reconstruction rule from
the supplied operational data can determine the target.

## Novelty Boundary

The note does not claim new Tomita--Takesaki theory, DHR/Doplicher--Roberts
reconstruction, split-property theory, locally covariant QFT, process
tomography, no-broadcasting, JLMS/OAQEC, gravitational crossed products, or a
new derivation of Bekenstein--Hawking entropy.  Those are treated as positive
inputs.

The claimed contribution is the converse/fiber classification: when those
positive inputs are quotiented, retracted, restricted, tested on a smaller
domain, or calibrated only up to a finite central residual, exactly which
central and entropy-normalization targets have fallen out of the data?

## Three Questions

1. Is the main iff criterion already present in the operator-algebra, AQFT, or
   quantum-information literature under another name?  If so, what is the exact
   theorem and citation?
2. Is any row of Theorem 1 false as stated, especially the rows for generated
   algebras versus spatial tensor products, sector-character retention, or
   entropy-density/Newton-coupling normalization?
3. Does the entropy-coefficient theorem require an additional analytic
   hypothesis on the area limit, trace normalization, or comparison class to be
   mathematically correct?
